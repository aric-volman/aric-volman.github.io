# About
This is a tierlist of my first year courses and some pros/cons + maybe descriptions.

## S+ Tier

### CSE 29
Pros:
- Relatively well-run course
- Lots of TAs, staff, and support
- Factually good overview of computing + the modern challenge of computing (memory and organization)
- PAs were straightforward
- Grading policy consistent and fair across all profs and quarters (all CSE education profs are nice)

Cons:
- (Opinion) For some reason lower-level is taught in the wrong order (I believe it should be logic -> assembly -> C)
- Very time consuming and high commitment
- Could have been C/C++ but instead pure C, why isn't C++ taught formally yet?
- Used as educational research test-bed (meaning weird grading policies)
- Could have had more content like traditional C programming courses 25 years ago...
- Could preach more Rust and Go :)

### WCWP 10A
Pros:
- Most profs hold the line on traditional revisions
- Guaranteed A
- Prompts provided enough flexibility such that students can write good essays

Cons:
- Too structured and rigid
- Presentations are good but not for every student
- Class discussions could go a little bit flat
- Weird program-wide rules like writing a written assignment

### WCWP 10B
Pros:
- Students get to choose topic (varies)
- Topic (American Dream) was the best topic and went very deep
- If you choose to, your essay can be very deep and dense
- Small enough class size and very engaging

Cons:
- Students get to choose topic (was my 2nd choice)
- The vaguer the topic, the more disorganized the course
- If you choose to, it can be time consuming
- Can feel physically brutal compared to your peers in Seventh College

## S Tier

### MATH 109
Pros:
- Eye-opening and really essential
- Diverse student body (All math majors + MAE + ECE)
- Mostly focused on logic and sets, very good for combinatorics
- Mathematical rigor really feels different from everything else
- Grading can be generous if needed

Cons:
- Existential crisis (you realize the same TAs will be here for a while until the funding situation is fixed)
- Make mistake on concept == bad grade on test (not a con retrospectively)
- Prof dependent/never truly standardized
- Homework grading can be hit or miss, do not get discouraged from feedback

### MATH 154
Pros:
- You learn induction so much better (induction for the strong!)
- Taught by math people for math people, current set of profs is good (can't go wrong with any)
- You will never stop talking about graph theory and never stop recommending it
- You get to learn about Paul Erdős
- Useful in networking, algorithms, maybe niche neural nets, NLP, etc...

Cons:
- Simultaneously difficult to use graph-theoretic concepts IRL
- Bang your head on induction (which requires practice)
- Hard HW but possible
- Easy midterm + harder final = a recipe for disaster (still got A-)

### MUS 6
Pros:
- You learn about Daft Punk, but only at the end
- You learn some things about music technology
- Current set of electronic music profs are really good
- Intersects with modern history quite a lot

Cons:
- Most of electronic music is esoteric by definition
- Essays were slightly hard and required historical context, hand-waving
- Should have been an enforced prereq for upper-div electronic music

## A Tier

### COGS 108
Pros:
- Teaches the feel of Python and basic machine learning
- Can be rigorous if you choose so
- Lectures are very good and informative on the consequences of technology
- Feels like a proper upper division class

Cons:
- NLP topic is unnecessary for 90% of students
- You can make the class as time-consuming or rigorous as possible
- Often found myself doing unnecessary assignments during lecture
- Hybrid between project and assignment class made it 2x more difficult
- Unnecessary duplication between assignments and discussion lab assignments (i.e. labeled A1 and D1)
- Incentives for students to take class must change (why can this be a GE for Marshall??)
- More ML topics == less time on each topic

### COGS 2
Pros:
- Good overview of how animals and humans learn (Idea: learning how to learn)
- Good course materials and good focus on the main ideas
- Great implications beyond just learning (i.e. theories of ADHD and Parkinson's disease)
- Well-run
- Discussion sections had a lot of time for chatting with friends :)

Cons:
- Very tight-ship compared to other courses
- Felt like AP Psychology all over again
- Topic can feel out-of-place for DEI and disconcerting (what is neuroeconomics??)
- You can tell that the university requirements are flawed (too many students took it just for DEI credit)

## B Tier

### PHYS 12
Pros:
- Prof is interesting man and is very passionate about topic (climate change)
- Nostalgia for York Hall in the evening
- Nostalgia for discussion sections (ran by prof, very awesome sections!!!)
- Discussion sections talked about futurism and technology, as well as SRM (solar radiation management) and other really interesting topics

Cons:
- Lecture could be boring
- Many students not engaged
- Not exactly physics, but still decent topic
- University requirement is pointless (We already know this.... it's obvious!!!)

### COGS 1
Pros:
- Gives broad perspective on Cogs department
- Most guest lecturers were great
- Gives the ideas, not the details

Cons:
- Assessment was difficult (lots of memorization)
- Discussion section had bad venue (CSB)
- Some guest lectures were asynchronous (which is a pro too)

### MATH 20C
Pros:
- Provides basics of multivariate
- Provides knowledge of Lagrange multipliers, very useful
- Provided me a taste on what math department is like
- Good vibrations/felt good

Cons:
- Multivariate is too basic to take seriously
- Linear algebra should be a prereq, both 20C and 20E could be combined
- Average student intelligence was quite shoddy at best
- Exams felt rather basic

## D Tier

### CSE 11
Pros:
- Good informative lectures, and quizzes were somewhat challenging
  
Cons:
- 2x as long PAs
- Redundant with AP CSA, assumes we are idiots
- 25% of the class got A+ for all of that wasted time and effort
- AI policy did not align with CSE 29/30 policy or other educational profs
- Zero connection to CSE department at all (burner/filler course)
- A waste of my time
- Java is weird (not quite Python, not quite C, not quite C++, somewhere in between and cursed)
